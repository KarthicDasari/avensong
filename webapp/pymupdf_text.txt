Avensong 2026 Amenity Access Packet 
December 01, 2025 
 
Dear Avensong Community Homeowners: 
 
Your access to the gated amenities will be turned off January 16, 2026, unless you have 
submitted the 2026 Amenity Access Packet and have made at least the minimum amount of the 
required 2026 HOA assessment payments.  
 
Please read the following information, complete the attached forms, and submit the entire 
packet as instructed. Unless you complete this process for 2026 your amenity card will not be 
activated. 
 
Once your packet is received, your HOA account(s) will be checked for eligibility. If you have not 
voted on the Covenant Amendment you will be asked to do so. You can find a copy of the 
proposed Amendment at www.avensong.org/ownerinfo. The passing of this Amendment is 
critical to the future well-being of Avensong.  
 
IMPORTANT: If you want access to the pool for Memorial Day weekend, you must submit the 
packet and have your homeowner account(s) in good standing BEFORE Friday, May 1, 2026. 
Due to the summer rush backlog, packets received after this date and/or delinquent accounts are 
not guaranteed pool access in time for the holiday weekend. 
 
You can submit your completed access packet in any of the following ways: 
 
 
Option 1: Email 
Scan, assemble and email the required pages in a SINGLE email to 
amenities@avensong.org.  
 Please ensure PDF scans are legible – illegible files will not be processed.   
 If emailing pictures, keep image file sizes around 1M each so they can be read for 
review and processing (illegible image files will be rejected). 
 Please send all pages/images in ONE email – nobody can sort through emails to find 
and assemble your access packet for you.  
 DO NOT SEND A LINK TO YOUR DOCUMENT. Avensong does not accept/access 
external links. 
 
 
Option 2: mail (USPS)  
If you cannot fax or email your packet, you can mail it to Avensong Community c/o 
Homeside Properties, Inc., 900 North Point Pkwy, Suite 325, Alpharetta, GA 30005.  
DO NOT BRING PACKETS TO HPI OFFICES - HPI DOES NOT ACCEPT IN-PERSON 
DROP-OFFS.  
 
 
If you have questions about this, please email amenities@avensong.org. We are happy to 
assist everyone during this process and hope we all have an enjoyable and safe 2026! 
 
 
Thank you, 
The Avensong HOA Board of Directors

Definitions 
 
Any term not defined herein shall have the meaning ascribed to it in the Declaration of Protective 
Covenants for Avensong recorded on August 14, 1995, in Deed Book 19602, Page 282, et seq. in the 
Fulton County, Georgia land records (the “Declaration”). Additionally, the following terms are defined 
for purposes of this Agreement: 
 
1. “Gathering” shall mean more than fifty (50) persons (or such number of persons set forth in an 
Executive Order issued by the Governor of the State of Georgia then in effect” physically present 
in a Single Location if, to be present, persons are required to stand or be seated within six (6) feet 
of any other person. 
2. “Single Location” shall mean a space where all persons gathered cannot maintain at least six (6) 
feet of distance between themselves and any other person.  
3. “Social Distancing” shall mean keeping space between yourself and other people outside of your 
home or place of residence. Persons practicing Social Distancing should stay at least six (6) feet 
from other people, avoid assembling in groups, avoid large, crowded places, and avoid large 
crowds. The provision shall not apply to cohabitating persons, family units, or roommates residing 
together in private homes, whether inside or outside of their homes or place of residence.

Additional Rules and Regulations 
 
 
The following Rules and Regulations have been adopted by the Board of Directors of the 
Association pertaining to the use of the Common Property, including the recreational facilities located 
thereon. The following Rules and Regulations are in addition to any restrictions contained in the 
Declaration, Association’s Bylaws or rules and regulations (“Association Legal Documents”). Any 
conflict between the following Rules and Regulations and the terms of the Association Legal Documents, 
the following Rules and Regulations shall control. The Releasor agrees to comply with the following Rules 
and Regulations and the terms of the Association Legal Documents and agrees to ensure all minor children 
that accompany him/her to the Common Property comply with the following Rules and Regulations and 
the terms of the Association Legal Documents.  
 
1. Individuals who have a fever or other symptoms of COIVD-19, or other infectious disease, shall 
not enter the common Property, including the recreations facilities located thereon. Symptoms of 
COVID_19, or other infectious diseases include, but are not limited to, a fever, cough, shortness 
of breath or difficulty breathing, chills muscle pain, sore throat or new loss of taste or smell. 
2. All individuals utilizing the Common Property shall do so at their own risk, being aware that any 
areas/surfaces that such individual comes into contact with are not disinfected after each use. 
Individuals are welcome to disinfect areas/surfaces before and after use if they wish.  
3. All individuals are encouraged to regularly wash their hands while at the Common Property. 
Residents with active amenity cards can access the resident bathrooms (under the clubhouse deck). 
4. Unnecessary person-to-person contact is discouraged. 
5. All persons who do not practice Social Distancing of non-cohabitating persons while present on 
the Common Property do so at their own risk. 
6. All individuals touching fencing, doors/gates, ladders, trash containers and other equipment and 
objects while utilizing the Common Property do so at their own risk. It is recommended that all 
individuals should use hand sanitizer and/or wash their hands after touching any of these items.  
7. Violation of these Rules and Regulations shall be reported immediately to the Association. 
8. No individual 18 years of age or older shall be permitted to enter the Common Property until such 
individual has executed this Agreement and delivered same to the Association. No individual 
under the age of 18 shall be permitted to enter the Common Property until the parent or guardian 
of such individual has executed this Agreement end delivered same to the Association. 
9. USE OF THE COMMON PROPERTY IS AT YOUR OWN RISK, INCLUDING THE RISK 
OF EXPOSURE TO CORONAVIRUS, SARS-COV-2, COVID-19, OR OTHER 
INFECTIOUS DIESEASES AND ILLNESSES. 
 
Violation Of these Rules and Regulations shall result in the imposition of fines and suspension of use 
privileges in accordance with the terms of the Association Legal Documents.

The following rules apply to all Avensong residents and their guests. 
(These rules are also posted at www.avensong.org/docs) 
All Avensong homeowner accounts must be in good standing (no balances, no violations) for amenity access. 
Common areas and gated amenities are for Avensong residents and their guests to enjoy. 
Avensong residents are responsible for their guests.  
Anyone violating these rules will be asked to leave and may be subject to fines.  
All amenity users agree to abide by any amenity rule updates, or use instructions, issued by email throughout the year.  
If your amenity card does not work, please contact amenities@avensong.org. 
 
Un-fenced Common Areas (playground, open spaces) 
 
 
No offensive language or disruptive/offensive behavior will be tolerated.  
 
No loud music – please keep the music at a level that does not disturb others. 
 
You may not use the amenities for personal or for-profit business.  
 
No smoking in/on/around the play structure or the area marked by the playground timbers. Please use the 
cigarette waste receptacle when done smoking. Do not throw lit cigarettes in the trash cans or landscaped 
areas.  
 
No alcoholic beverages in/on/around the play structure or the area of the playground timbers. Drinking alcoholic 
beverages in public is subject to the laws of the City of Milton.  
 
Please dispose of all trash in the trash cans around the common areas. Littering is subject to automatic fines. 
 
No overnight parking in the clubhouse parking lot without prior HOA approval.  
 
Questions about the amenities can be emailed to amenities@avensong.org.  
 
 
Clubhouse Rental 
Please visit the “Clubhouse Rental” page on the www.avensong.org website for full information regarding the rental of 
the clubhouse for a personal event. 
 
The clubhouse (and deck) may be rented for personal events by resident homeowners or tenants only (off-site investors 
are not permitted amenity access).  
 NOTE: The homeowner is responsible for any damage to the clubhouse or other common areas as a result of 
the clubhouse rental agreement made under their property address. 
 
 
The clubhouse rental includes the clubhouse and use of the deck only.  
o Gated amenities (tennis courts, basketball court and pool) are not included. Clubhouse rental guests who 
use the gated amenities during an event will forfeit the entirety of their clubhouse rental deposit. 
o The playground and other common areas are not included in the rental and are “public “use” spaces. 
Clubhouse renters may not restrict access to these areas to anyone during their rental or will forfeit the 
entirety of their clubhouse rental deposit. 
 
The clubhouse rental application and all deposits must be received before anyone will be given access to the 
clubhouse. 
 
All rules for the clubhouse must be adhered to. Please note that additional fines/penalties may be assessed if the 
clubhouse and deck are not returned in the same or better condition than received.  
 
All fines will be assessed to the homeowner account listed on the Clubhouse Rental Agreement.

Gated Amenities (pool, tennis courts, basketball court, bathrooms) 
 
While using the gated amenities, upon request you may be asked to demonstrate your amenity card works at a 
gate or you will be asked to leave.  
 
Pedestrian gates, tennis court gates, basketball court gate, the pool gate and bathroom doors must NOT to be 
propped open or climbed on. 
 
Active cardholders who allow non-cardholders into the fenced amenities are responsible for the non-
cardholder’s behavior.  
 
Gated amenity violations are issued per calendar year. 
These penalties are subject to variation depending on the severity of the violation. 
o First violation = deactivation of all amenity cards for 2 weeks 
o Second violation = $50 fine and deactivation of all amenity cards for 4 weeks 
o Third violation = $100 fine and deactivation of all amenity cards for the remainder of the calendar year 
 
Tennis & Pickleball Courts 
The tennis courts are for the use and enjoyment of Avensong residents and their guests from 6:30am until 9pm daily.  
 
 
Users are required to check “Reserve My Court” and must vacate immediately if a court is reserved in the app. 
 
If not reserved, the courts are available on a first come/first-serve basis.  
 
Residents and their guests may occupy up to two (2) courts at a time. In the event other residents are waiting to 
use a guest-occupied court, the court must be vacated at the end of the current game (not set or match). 
 
You may not use the courts for personal or for-profit business. Private lessons are permitted for members of the 
same household on a single court only. Group lessons are not permitted. 
 
Tennis court gates are not to be propped open. If your amenity card does not work, you must contact 
amenities@avensong.org to find out why. 
 
No smoking inside the tennis courts. 
 
No glass containers inside the tennis court gates.  
 
No loud music inside the tennis courts.  
 
Animals are not allowed inside the tennis court fenced area at any time. 
 
“Ride-on” toys (bikes, skateboards, scooters, skates, etc.) are not permitted on the courts at any time. 
 
Tennis court nets and fencing are not to be climbed on. 
 
Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult over the age of 18. 
 
 
Basketball Court

 
The court is available on a first come/first-serve basis.  
 
You may not use the court for personal or for-profit business.  
 
The basketball court gate is not to be propped open. If your amenity card does not work, you must contact 
amenities@avensong.org to find out why. 
 
No smoking inside the basketball court. 
 
No glass containers inside the basketball court 
 
No loud music is allowed in/around the basketball court. 


 
“Ride-on” toys (bikes, skateboards, scooters, skates, etc.) are not permitted on the court.

Pool 
The Avensong Pool is for residents of Avensong and their guests only. 
Everyone at the pool is responsible for keeping the pool fun and safe for all Avensong residents. 
Anyone violating these rules will be asked to leave and is subject to card deactivation and fines.  
The pool company employees have the full authority of the Avensong HOA Board of Directors to act on all violations. 
 
The following pool rules apply to all Avensong residents and their guests. 
 
 
NOTE: There is NO lifeguard on duty. Swim at your own risk! 
 
The pool will be open to Avensong RESIDENTS with valid amenity cards only. Current year pool hours are posted 
by the pool gate. 2026 daily operating hours are 6:300AM until 8:00PM. 
 
The pool will be closed to bathers for 30 minutes at the direction of the gate attendant for maintenance and 
cleaning. Any resident that does not leave the pool when asked by the gate attendant will be subject to fines 
and loss of pool access. 
 
You must have your valid amenity card to use the pool. If your amenity card does not work, you must contact 
amenities@avensong.org to find out why. 
 
Bathers without a valid amenity card will be considered trespassing and the police may be called.  
 
Do not open the gate for people without an active amenity card (there’s a reason they don’t have one).  
 
Jumping over/climbing on the fence to access the pool is not permitted.   
 
Residents may not share their cards with other residents (there’s a reason they don’t have one). 
 
Residents of Avensong are not permitted to be “guests” of another resident. 
 
Residents with a valid amenity card may bring up to (4) non-resident guests, of any age to the pool area.  
 
No parties at the pool (remember, only 4 guests per card). 
 
You may not use the pool for personal or for-profit business.  
 
Private swimming lessons are permitted for members of the same household. Instructors must enter and leave 
the pool area with you. The instructor may not conduct group lessons using the Avensong pool.   
 
Pedestrian gates, the pool gate and bathroom doors must NOT to be propped open. 
 
Proper swim attire is required. Children who are not fully toilet trained must use swim diapers. 
 
Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult over the age of 18. 
 
Do not put pool furniture (chairs, umbrellas, tables) in the pool. 
 
No offensive language or disruptive/offensive behavior will be tolerated.  
 
No smoking inside any of the gated areas. 
 
No alcoholic beverages inside any of the gated areas. 
 
No skateboards, bikes, roller blades or other “ride-on” toys are allowed in the fenced pool area. 
 
No running or “rough-house” play is allowed in the fenced pool area. 
 
No glass containers inside the fenced pool area. 
 
No pets/animals inside the fenced pool area. 
 
No loud music – please keep the music at a level that does not disturb others or use headphones. 
 
 “Pool toys” only – no footballs, baseballs, Frisbees or other hard toys allowed inside the fenced pool area.
